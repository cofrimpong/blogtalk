# The Architecture of Clear Thinking

Clear thinking is rarely accidental. It is typically the product of structure.

When teams feel "stuck," the issue is often not a lack of intelligence; it is a lack of shared architecture for reasoning.

## Three layers

### 1) Problem framing

A poorly framed problem cannot be solved elegantly.

- Define the objective function.
- Define constraints explicitly.
- Separate symptoms from root causes.

### 2) Model selection

Use the simplest model that captures the critical dynamics.

- First-order effects are mandatory.
- Second-order effects are strategic.
- Third-order effects are where many expensive mistakes hide.

### 3) Communication

If reasoning cannot survive contact with others, it is not operational.

- Distill to one-page briefs.
- Show assumptions and confidence levels.
- Separate facts, inferences, and recommendations.

Precision of thought compounds into precision of execution.
