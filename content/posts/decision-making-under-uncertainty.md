# Decision-Making Under Uncertainty

Most consequential decisions are made with partial information. Waiting for perfect certainty is often just a sophisticated form of avoidance.

The goal is not to eliminate uncertainty. The goal is to **improve decision quality despite uncertainty**.

## A simple operating model

1. **Define the reversible vs irreversible split.**
   - Reversible decisions should be made quickly with lightweight rigor.
   - Irreversible decisions deserve deeper analysis and explicit downside protection.
2. **Name your base rates before your narrative.**
   - Historical frequencies beat confident stories.
   - Start with outside view, then adjust with inside context.
3. **Write the kill criteria in advance.**
   - What evidence would prove this was wrong?
   - When do we stop, pivot, or double down?

## The quality test

A decision is high quality if it is:

- **Legible**: assumptions are explicit,
- **Auditable**: trade-offs are recorded,
- **Adaptive**: feedback loops are built in.

Outcomes are noisy. Decision quality is a discipline.
